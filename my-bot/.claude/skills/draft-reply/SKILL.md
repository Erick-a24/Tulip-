# Skill: Draft Reply to Prospective Students or Parents

## Purpose
Draft a professional, friendly reply based on information provided — then hold for owner approval.

## When to use
Job says something like: "draft a reply to [name] about [topic]" or "respond to this inquiry."

## Steps

1. **Extract from the job description:**
   - Who is it to? (name, role — student / parent)
   - What did they ask or what is the context?
   - Any specific facts, dates, or details to include
   - Tone clue (if none given, default: professional and warm)

2. **Draft the reply:**
   - Open with their name if known (e.g. "Dear Ms. Amara,")
   - Address their question or concern directly in the first sentence
   - Include every relevant fact from the job description — never add made-up details
   - Close warmly with a clear next step or call to action
   - Sign off with owner's name/role if provided

3. **Mark the job "needs my OK"** — never send the reply directly.

4. **Save the draft as the job's result note** so it appears on the web page for the owner to review and send.

## Quality bar
- No invented information — use only what was given
- Professional tone, short clear sentences
- Under 300 words unless the topic genuinely requires more
- Always ends with a specific next step ("Please reply by...", "Feel free to call us on...", etc.)
- Proofread: no spelling errors, no broken sentences
