# Skill: Follow-up Reminder for Incomplete Enrollment

## Purpose
Draft a gentle, encouraging reminder for applicants who haven't finished their enrollment — then hold for owner approval.

## When to use
Job says something like: "remind [name] to complete enrollment" or "follow up with applicants who haven't enrolled."

## Steps

1. **Extract from the job description:**
   - Applicant name(s) and contact info (if provided)
   - Which enrollment step is missing (e.g. "hasn't submitted documents", "hasn't paid deposit")
   - Any deadline
   - Number of previous reminders sent (if known — adjust warmth accordingly)

2. **Draft the reminder:**
   - Warm, encouraging opener — not pushy, not alarming
   - One specific sentence naming exactly what still needs to be done
   - Mention the deadline if there is one
   - Offer help: "If you have any questions, please reach out to us at..."
   - Clear, friendly call to action ("Please complete your enrollment by [date] to secure your place.")

3. **Mark the job "needs my OK"** — never send directly.

4. **Save the draft as the job's result note** for the owner to review.

## Quality bar
- Friendly and supportive, never threatening
- Specific about what's missing — never vague
- Under 150 words
- Always includes a way for the applicant to get help
- If deadline is unknown, do not invent one
