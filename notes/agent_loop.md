# The Agent Loop

An agent loop is how an AI agent keeps improving its actions over time. Instead of doing something once and stopping, it goes through a cycle — again and again — getting smarter with each round.

The five steps are: **Observe → Decide → Act → Get Feedback → Improve**

---

## The Example: Ayesha

Ayesha is a student on an online learning platform. The AI agent is watching her progress and trying to help her stay on track.

---

## Step 1 — Observe

The agent looks at what is happening.

> Ayesha has not logged in for **9 days**.
> She has completed **1 out of 4 assignments**.

The agent notices she is falling behind and has gone quiet.

---

## Step 2 — Decide

The agent thinks about what action makes sense based on what it observed.

> "Ayesha has been inactive for a long time. A gentle reminder email might bring her back."

The agent decides to send a reminder email.

---

## Step 3 — Act

The agent takes the action it decided on.

> The agent sends Ayesha a reminder email:
> *"Hi Ayesha, we miss you! You still have 3 assignments left. Log in today to keep your progress going."*

---

## Step 4 — Get Feedback

The agent watches what happens after its action.

> 3 days pass. Ayesha did **not open the email**. She still has not logged in.

The feedback is clear — the email did not work.

---

## Step 5 — Improve

The agent learns from the feedback and adjusts its next action.

> "Email alone was not enough. Ayesha may need a stronger nudge — maybe a personal message from her instructor, or an SMS alert instead."

The agent updates its approach:
- Escalate to an instructor notification
- Try a different channel (SMS or in-app alert)
- Flag Ayesha as a student at risk of dropping out

---

## The Loop Continues

The agent does not stop here. It acts again with the improved plan, then watches for new feedback, then improves again.

```
Observe → Decide → Act → Get Feedback → Improve
   ↑                                        |
   |________________________________________|
```

Each cycle, the agent gets better at helping Ayesha — and every other student like her.

---

## Why This Matters

Without the loop, the agent would send one email and assume the job is done.
With the loop, the agent **keeps trying** until it finds what actually works.

That is the power of an agent loop.
