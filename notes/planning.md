# Planning
# 3-Month School Storytelling Content Strategy — Planning Document
### Silverleaf Academy Media & Marketing Department

---

## OVERVIEW

This document breaks down the production of a comprehensive 3-Month Storytelling Content Strategy into clearly defined stages, actions, expected outputs, and considerations. It serves as the internal project plan for building the final deliverable: a Media Department Operating Manual.

---

## STAGE 1 — STRATEGIC FOUNDATION
**Duration:** Days 1–3
**Purpose:** Establish the "why" before anything is created. Align the entire content strategy to school goals.

### Actions
1. **Define the school's marketing objectives** for the 3-month period:
   - Enrollment targets (new student acquisition)
   - Retention goals (re-enrollment signals for existing families)
   - Brand awareness in the Arusha community
   - Staff morale and recruitment
   - Alumni engagement

2. **Identify the primary and secondary audiences** for each content pillar:
   - Prospective parents (highest priority for enrollment)
   - Current parents (retention and word-of-mouth)
   - Students (pride, motivation, belonging)
   - Staff and teachers (morale and professional identity)
   - Community stakeholders and alumni
   - Prospective staff (recruitment)

3. **Map the school's core brand values** — the emotional and narrative territory every piece of content must reinforce:
   - Academic rigor and excellence
   - Nurturing, child-centered environment
   - Holistic development (mind, body, character)
   - Community and belonging
   - Innovation and future readiness

4. **Choose the 3-month window** and align it to the school calendar:
   - Identify term dates, major events, sports fixtures, trips, exams, and celebrations
   - Flag seasonal storytelling opportunities (rains, school year milestones, national holidays)

5. **Audit existing content** to understand what has been captured before and what gaps exist.

### Expected Outputs
- A one-page Strategic Brief summarizing goals, audiences, and brand values
- A populated school calendar for the 3-month period
- A content gap analysis (what stories have never been told)

### Considerations
- Involve the school principal and admissions team in setting objectives — this is not just a creative exercise
- Ensure brand values are authentic and grounded in real school life, not aspirational fiction
- The Silverleaf brand palette and tone guide should be referenced throughout

## STAGE 2 — CONTENT PILLAR FRAMEWORK
**Duration:** Days 4–7
**Purpose:** Build the full library of storytelling pillars that will structure all content planning.

### Actions
1. **Define all 21 content pillars** (as outlined in the brief). For each pillar:
   - Write a clear 2-3 sentence strategic purpose statement
   - Identify the core emotion the content should generate (e.g., pride, trust, inspiration, warmth, belonging)
   - Name the primary audience
   - List 5–10 specific capturable moments unique to that pillar

2. **Assign pillar weights** — not all pillars are equal. Rank them by:
   - Impact on enrollment
   - Frequency of occurrence
   - Emotional resonance
   - Platform suitability

3. **Group pillars into narrative families** to allow thematic content weeks:
   - **Learning & Growth:** Academic excellence, classroom learning, science/innovation, future readiness
   - **People & Character:** Teacher impact, student growth, leadership, character development
   - **Community & Culture:** School culture, parent engagement, alumni, community service
   - **Talent & Expression:** Performing arts, co-curricular, clubs, sports
   - **School Life:** Daily routines, boarding, facilities, trips, events

4. **Write a "Story Brief" for each pillar** containing:
   - Strategic purpose
   - Emotions to evoke
   - Target audience
   - Specific capture moments
   - Photo examples
   - Video examples
   - Interview and testimonial opportunities
   - Reel ideas
   - Long-form video opportunities
   - Carousel ideas
   - Seasonal relevance

### Expected Outputs
- 21 fully written Story Briefs (one per pillar)
- A pillar priority ranking with rationale
- A narrative family grouping map

### Considerations
- Each Story Brief must go beyond the obvious. Push the team to find the hidden moment within each pillar (e.g., in "Sports," the hidden moment is not the winning goal — it is the team huddle before kick-off, the student wrapping their own ankle, the coach's quiet word after a loss)
- Briefs should be written in language the media team (photographers, videographers, editors) can understand and act on — not marketing jargon

## STAGE 3 — "NEVER MISS MOMENTS" LIBRARY
**Duration:** Days 6–8 (overlapping with Stage 2)
**Purpose:** Build the definitive catalogue of overlooked, spontaneous, and high-impact moments that no school media team should ever walk past.

### Actions
1. **Brainstorm and document 100+ specific micro-moments** organised by location:
   - Classrooms (e.g., student's face when a concept finally clicks)
   - Playground (e.g., cross-age friendships between older and younger students)
   - Assembly hall (e.g., student visibly moved by a peer's speech)
   - Sports fields (e.g., teammate picking up an injured player)
   - Science labs (e.g., the moment an experiment produces an unexpected result)
   - Library (e.g., student absorbed in a book, surrounded by morning light)
   - Dining hall (e.g., students teaching each other something informally over lunch)
   - Corridors (e.g., a teacher stopping to acknowledge a student's artwork on the wall)
   - Gates at arrival (e.g., the first-day nervous parent watching their child disappear into class)
   - Boarding house (e.g., students helping each other study after lights-out)
   - Backstage before performances (e.g., students calming each other's nerves)
   - Trips and excursions (e.g., student encountering something for the first time)

2. **Tag each moment** with:
   - Which content pillar it supports
   - Which platform it is best suited for
   - Whether it requires consent or special sensitivity

3. **Flag "Golden Window Moments"** — moments that only happen once and can never be staged (e.g., reaction to exam results, a student's first competition win).

### Expected Outputs
- A "Never Miss Moments" library of 100+ entries
- Tagged and organised by location, pillar, and platform
- A "Golden Window Moments" alert list for the media team

### Considerations
- Some of these moments involve minors in emotional states — establish clear ethical and consent guidelines upfront
- Moments that are sensitive (e.g., a child crying after losing) require discretion; document the policy for when to put the camera down

## STAGE 4 — PLATFORM & FORMAT STRATEGY
**Duration:** Days 8–10
**Purpose:** Ensure every story is delivered in the format and on the platform where it will have maximum impact.

### Actions
1. **Define the school's active platforms** and their role:
   - **Instagram:** Visual storytelling, reels, daily school life, parent engagement — primary platform
   - **Facebook:** Community building, event coverage, parent-facing long-form updates, sharing
   - **LinkedIn:** Professional credibility, teacher/leadership stories, school milestones, alumni
   - **TikTok:** Student life, fun behind-the-scenes, reach with younger prospective families
   - **YouTube:** Long-form documentaries, event highlights, virtual tours, term reviews
   - **School Website:** Evergreen content, blog posts, photo galleries, admissions support

2. **Create a content format menu** — for each story type, define the ideal formats:
   - Single photo post with caption narrative
   - Photo carousel (3–10 images with a story arc)
   - 15–30 second Reel (fast, emotional, music-driven)
   - 60–90 second mini-documentary style video
   - 3–8 minute long-form YouTube film
   - Written blog post supported by photography
   - Parent testimonial video (60–120 seconds)
   - Student spotlight series
   - Staff feature series

3. **Map each content pillar to its best format and platform combinations.**

4. **Create repurposing pathways** — a single event should produce:
   - 1 long-form YouTube video
   - 3–5 Instagram posts or carousels
   - 1–2 Reels
   - 1 Facebook post
   - 1 LinkedIn post (if professionally relevant)
   - 1 website gallery or blog post

### Expected Outputs
- Platform role definitions document
- Content format menu
- Pillar-to-platform mapping matrix
- Repurposing pathway templates for major event types

### Considerations
- Platform strategies must match the school's actual team capacity — if only one person manages social media, the strategy must be realistic
- Video content requires post-production time; build this into the content calendar

## STAGE 5 — MONTHLY & WEEKLY CONTENT CALENDAR
**Duration:** Days 10–15
**Purpose:** Turn the storytelling framework into an actionable, day-by-day operational calendar for 3 months.

### Actions
1. **Build a Month-by-Month Overview** for each of the 3 months:
   - Identify the flagship story/theme of the month
   - List all major school events, sports fixtures, co-curricular milestones
   - Assign each week a narrative focus aligned to the pillar families
   - Flag seasonal or calendar-relevant hooks (e.g., national holidays, term transitions)

2. **Build a Weekly Content Template** showing:
   - Monday: Motivational/academic content (sets the week's tone)
   - Tuesday: Teacher or staff spotlight
   - Wednesday: Student story or achievement
   - Thursday: Co-curricular/sport/club content
   - Friday: Behind-the-scenes or community/culture content
   - Weekend: Event coverage or curated parent engagement content

3. **For each week, specify:**
   - 1 flagship story to chase (planned capture)
   - 2–3 recurring content slots (template-driven)
   - 1 "spontaneous capture" prompt — a type of hidden moment to look for
   - Platform distribution plan

4. **Create Event Coverage Plans** for every major event in the 3-month window:
   - Pre-event build-up content (anticipation storytelling)
   - Day-of shot list (must-have photos and videos)
   - Post-event wrap-up content (reflection and community response)

5. **Create the Sports Coverage Calendar:**
   - Pre-match training captures
   - Match-day shot list (specific moments to look for)
   - Post-match emotional storytelling
   - Season narrative arc (team journey over 3 months)

6. **Create the Trips & Excursions Coverage Plan:**
   - Pre-trip excitement and preparation
   - Departure and travel moments
   - Learning and discovery moments on location
   - Return and reflection content

### Expected Outputs
- 3-month master content calendar (week-by-week view)
- Weekly content template
- Event coverage plans for all major events
- Sports season coverage arc
- Trips and excursions coverage plan

### Considerations
- Build buffer capacity into the calendar — do not fill every day; leave space for reactive content from unexpected great moments
- The calendar must be shared with teachers, sports coaches, and activity coordinators so they can alert the media team in advance

## STAGE 6 — SHOT LISTS & CAPTURE CHECKLISTS
**Duration:** Days 14–18
**Purpose:** Give photographers and videographers specific, actionable guidance for every category of coverage.

### Actions
1. **Create Master Shot Lists** for recurring event types:
   - School assembly
   - Sports match (team sports)
   - Individual sports competition
   - Drama or music performance
   - Science fair or exhibition
   - Educational trip
   - Club meeting
   - Inter-school competition
   - Prize-giving or awards ceremony
   - Parent open day or enrollment event
   - Classroom lesson (regular day)
   - Morning arrival

2. **For each shot list, structure into four categories:**
   - **Must-Have Photos** (non-negotiable captures)
   - **Must-Have Videos** (non-negotiable footage)
   - **Behind-the-Scenes Content** (preparation, waiting, quiet moments)
   - **Emotional Storytelling Moments** (the human-interest layer)

3. **Create Interview Prompt Cards** for each pillar — a list of 5–8 questions a content creator can ask a student, teacher, or parent on camera to generate authentic testimonial content.

4. **Create a Daily Capture Checklist** — a short daily prompt card for the media team showing what to look for each day, tied to the weekly theme.

### Expected Outputs
- 12+ master shot lists for recurring event types
- Interview prompt cards for students, teachers, and parents
- Daily capture checklist template
- Behind-the-scenes capture guide

### Considerations
- Shot lists should be printed as laminated cards the photographer carries — not buried in a document
- Video shot lists must specify minimum duration for each clip (e.g., "hold for at least 5 seconds before moving")
- All interviews with minors require parent consent forms in place before filming

## STAGE 7 — CONTENT WORKFLOW & OPERATING SYSTEM
**Duration:** Days 17–21
**Purpose:** Transform the team from reactive to proactive by building a clear operational system for planning, capturing, organising, and publishing content.

### Actions
1. **Design the Weekly Planning Ritual:**
   - Every Friday: media team reviews next week's calendar, assigns coverage responsibilities, prepares shot lists and interview prompts
   - Every Monday: team confirms the week's flagship story and spontaneous capture focus
   - Daily: 10-minute check-in on what was captured, what was missed, what is coming tomorrow

2. **Build the Content Organisation System:**
   - Folder structure for storing all media: `/Year/Month/Week/Pillar/Event`
   - File naming convention: `YYYY-MM-DD_Pillar_Description_Format` (e.g., `2025-09-12_Sports_FootballMatchday_Reel.mp4`)
   - Tagging system for searchability (person, location, pillar, platform, status)
   - Content status labels: Captured → Reviewed → Edited → Approved → Published → Archived

3. **Create the Content Approval Workflow:**
   - Photographer/videographer submits raw content within 24 hours
   - Content lead reviews for quality and story alignment
   - Principal or designated approver signs off on any sensitive content
   - Editor produces final version within 48 hours of shoot
   - Social media manager schedules and publishes

4. **Build the Repurposing System:**
   - Every event generates a "content harvest" checklist — all formats that must be produced from each piece of raw footage
   - Monthly content audit: review what was captured, what was used, what was wasted, what should have been captured

5. **Create the Storytelling Brief Template** — a one-page form completed before every planned capture:
   - What story are we telling?
   - Who is the audience?
   - What emotion should this create?
   - What platform is this for?
   - What is the one image or clip that must exist when this is done?

### Expected Outputs
- Weekly planning ritual guide
- Folder structure and file naming convention guide
- Content status tracking system
- Approval workflow diagram
- Repurposing checklist template
- Pre-capture Storytelling Brief template

### Considerations
- The workflow must be realistic for the current team size — scale complexity to capacity
- Cloud-based storage (Google Drive or similar) is strongly recommended for collaboration
- Build redundancy: if the primary photographer is absent, a backup must have access to the shot list

## STAGE 8 — DOCUMENT ASSEMBLY & FORMATTING
**Duration:** Days 20–25
**Purpose:** Compile all outputs into the final Media Department Operating Manual.

### Actions
1. **Structure the final document** with the following sections:
   - Executive Summary (1 page)
   - Strategic Foundation (audience, goals, brand values)
   - The 21 Content Pillars (full Story Briefs)
   - Never Miss Moments Library (100+ entries)
   - Platform & Format Strategy
   - 3-Month Content Calendar (visual and detailed)
   - Shot Lists & Capture Checklists
   - Content Workflow & Operating System
   - Templates Appendix (Storytelling Brief, Capture Checklist, Interview Prompts, File Naming Guide)

2. **Format for usability:**
   - Use clear headings, sub-headings, and icons for navigation
   - Create a visual summary calendar (one-page monthly overview)
   - Print-ready shot list cards in a separate appendix
   - Tables for platform-pillar mapping

3. **Create a visual version** of the content calendar suitable for display in the media room.

4. **Produce a quick-reference "Pocket Guide"** — a 2-page summary the media team carries daily.

### Expected Outputs
- Full Media Department Operating Manual (master document)
- Visual 3-month content calendar (one-page-per-month format)
- Print-ready shot list cards
- Pocket Guide (2-page quick reference)

### Considerations
- The document should be saved in both editable and PDF formats
- Use Silverleaf Academy's brand colours and fonts throughout
- The document should be reviewed by the Head of Marketing and one senior teacher before being finalised

## STAGE 9 — TEAM ONBOARDING & ROLLOUT
**Duration:** Days 25–28
**Purpose:** Ensure the media team understands, believes in, and can execute the strategy independently.

### Actions
1. **Run a half-day team briefing** covering:
   - The strategic purpose behind the plan
   - How to use the shot lists and weekly calendar
   - How to identify and capture hidden moments
   - The filing, naming, and workflow system
   - How to conduct a good on-camera interview

2. **Conduct a test run** — assign the team a low-stakes upcoming event to cover using the new system. Review the output together.

3. **Create a feedback loop** — at the end of each month, review what content worked, what was missed, what should be adjusted for next month.

4. **Identify a Content Champion** on the team — one person responsible for upholding the storytelling standards, running the weekly planning ritual, and maintaining the content archive.

### Expected Outputs
- Team briefing slide deck (or printed guide)
- Test run debrief notes
- Month-end review template
- Content Champion role description

### Considerations
- Adoption is more important than perfection — the first month will be imperfect and that is acceptable
- Recognition for great content captures motivates the team; celebrate wins publicly
- The strategy should be treated as a living document, updated each term

## KEY CROSS-CUTTING CONSIDERATIONS

### Consent & Child Safety
- All photography and videography of students requires valid consent on file
- Sensitive moments (tears, conflict, distress) must never be published without specific approval
- A clear policy must exist: when does the camera stay in the bag?

### Authenticity Over Polish
- Parents trust authentic imperfect moments far more than staged perfection
- The strategy should lean toward genuine school life, not a promotional brochure
- Avoid over-direction of students for the camera

### Consistency Over Virality
- The goal is not one viral post; it is 90 days of consistent, purposeful storytelling
- A predictable content rhythm builds audience trust and platform algorithm favour

### Measurement & Accountability
- Define 3–5 key metrics to track across the 3 months (e.g., Instagram reach, website gallery visits, inquiry form submissions attributed to social media)
- Review metrics monthly and adjust content mix accordingly

### Localization
- Content should reflect Arusha, Tanzania authentically — the environment, community, and culture are a competitive advantage, not a backdrop
- Swahili captions or bilingual content may increase reach with local prospective families
